Hailey Trampel

Young Life

Hailey was born on March 11, 2001 to Tom and Cindy Trampel. She is the youngest of 4: an oldest brother Tommy, and two older sisters Sabrina and Shannon. She grew up in West Chester, PA, close to Philadelphia.

She was always an animal-lover and even asked Santa on live radio for a dog--Daisy, a boxer who she got that year for Christmas! She always wanted to be a vet when she was older, but things didn't go as planned! Her senior year of high school, right after she got accepted to Pitt, her family adopted a boxer-pug mix named Josie.

College

Hailey now attends the University of Pittsburgh. She is a double major in computer science and information science. She is also an undergraduate teaching assistant for Intermediate Programming with Java! She has worked with man languages throughout her experience with computer science, including Java, SQL, C, HTML, and Python. This semester, she is working as a Research Assistant for a project in which she is training artificial intelligence for an application being produced by another university.

Passions and Hobbies

Hailey's biggest passion for as long as she can remember is music. As the youngest of four, she was surrounded by a variety of music tastes and found herself loving the variety. Her and her dad bonded over a passion for music from when he was younger: The Clash, The Cure, The Doors, Elvis Costello, Elton John, David Bowie, Van Halen, and more. She continues that passion by learning more about artists that have transformed the music world from generation to generation. She loves sharing her music with her close friends and family, especially when she finds a new artist she loves.

As aforementioned, Hailey also has a passion for animals. She grew up with two boxers, Daisy and Duke. She then became really passionate about animal rescue and that is when she fell in love with Josie, a boxer-pug mix who was found as a stray on the streets of Alabama and given a "freedom flight" to the West Chester SPCA. Hailey has since volunteered at local pet rescues.
